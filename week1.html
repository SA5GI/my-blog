Week 1

What Went Well

In week one, the sprint goal was to learn how to use a GitHub repository and successfully launch a basic blog using Netlify. We were also asked to revisit our HTML, CSS, and JavaScript skills by creating a cupcake website. Another key task was to generate three problem statements and narrow them down to one. Finally, we needed to create and publish a blog post on our live Netlify site. Overall, I felt quite positive about how the week went. I was able to conduct research into potential problem areas, which helped me understand whether the issues I was considering were realistic and achievable within the 8-week timeframe. ChatGPT played a major role in this process — I used its deep research feature to explore a wide range of sources, which gave me helpful insights in just a few minutes. Setting up Git and launching the website on Netlify was surprisingly smooth. I figured everything out quickly and was satisfied with the outcome, as I didn’t run into any major issues. Creating the problem statements was also relatively easy. There are so many real-world issues to tackle, but it takes creative thinking to approach them in a way that hasn’t already been done. Building the weekly blog felt simple at this early stage since it's still very basic. Now that I’m writing this reflection, I feel more grounded in the sprint process and understand what I’ve done and what still needs attention.

What Didn’t Go Well

One area that didn’t go well was my attempt to recap HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. I don’t feel confident in my coding skills yet, and it’s something I find frustrating. Still, I’ve been trying to stick with it by reminding myself that sometimes you need to embrace what you dislike in order to grow — and that mindset has helped a bit. Staying focused has also been a challenge. I find myself easily distracted and able to spend hours doing something like gardening, but struggling to sit down and work on university tasks. While I did come up with problem statements, I’m still unsure about which one to pursue. I feel overwhelmed by the amount of information I consume through media like Reddit and YouTube, which clutters my thoughts. However, I’ve found that meditating before lectures really helps clear my mind and improve my focus — so I’ll continue doing that.

What Can Be Improved

I need to finalize my problem statement and make sure it connects to the idea of reducing information overload and helping the mind shift away from constant digital input. My schedule also needs to be more consistent. Right now, I’m too flexible with my sleep and daily routine, and it’s affecting my productivity. Most importantly, I need to strengthen my understanding of JavaScript and CSS. I often feel lost in class and behind on the techniques we’re learning, which sometimes makes me want to give up. But I’ve learned that pushing through discomfort is part of the journey, and I’m working on building more discipline and reducing procrastination.
